About Us

RISE THE GREEN POLYMERS HAS BEEN ESTIBLISHED AS A PIONEER IN THE FIELD OF TOTAL WASTE MANAGEMENT COMPANY IN HYDERABAD. Established in 23 April 2024, Rise the Green Polymers is a Hyderabad based company that recycles all types of plastic waste. Founded by Mr. Vatupalli Sivaji & Mr. Kokkiligadda Prasanth, our company is dedicated to recycling all types of plastic waste, setting the standard for environmental responsibility and sustainability. Rise the Green Polymers, we are committed to our mission of creating a cleaner and safer environment for future generations and for Mother Earth. Spearheaded by leaders who exhibit unwavering passion and accountability, we strive to revolutionize waste management practices. We specialize in manufacturing various reprocessed plastic granules from waste or scrap materials, marking a significant step towards mitigating plastic pollution and promoting a circular economy.

Polymers are all around us, shaping modern life in ways we often take for granted. From the plastic containers in our kitchens to the synthetic fibers in our clothes, these materials play a crucial role in various industries. But what exactly are polymers, and where do we find them in daily life.

Polymers usage

Polymers are widely used in various industries and everyday products, ranging from packaging materials to medical devices and textiles.

Polymers are integral to modern life, with applications spanning numerous fields. Their versatility, durability, and adaptability make them essential materials in both everyday products and advanced technologies. As research continues, the potential uses of polymers are likely to expand even further. A polymer is a large molecule composed of repeated subunits, known as monomers. Polymers are an outcome of a process called polymer synthesis. These materials are used in everyday life in the form of plastics, petrochemicals and bio-based feedstocks, making for an industry worth ₹3.65 lakh crore with a CAGR projected at over 6.6%. This growth is responsible for the surge in demand, especially in sectors such as packaging, automotive, infrastructure and FMCG.
